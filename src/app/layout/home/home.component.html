SYMBOLS OF CARICOM

  • The CARICOM LOGO

    The two Cs are for the words “Caribbean Community” (CARICOM). They are in the form of broken links in a chain and symbolise the 15 countries linked together by their common history, culture, aspiration and integration and by their break from the colonial past.

  • The CARICOM Standard

    - The CARICOM Standard features a blue background. The upper part is light blue and represents the sky; the lower part is dark blue and represents the Caribbean Sea.

    - The yellow circle in the centre represents the sun.

    - The black interlocking Cs is the logo of the Caribbean Community.

    -The narrow ring of green around the sun represents the vegetation of the CARICOM Region.

    -The Fourth Conference of Heads of Government Meeting in Port-of-Spain, Trinidad and Tobago, in July 1983, approved the design of the CARICOM Standard.

    -The original design was done by the firm of WINART Studies in Georgetown, Guyana.

    -The CARICOM Standard was flown for the first time at the Fifth Conference of Heads of Government Meeting in Nassau, The Bahamas, in July, 1984.”.

  • -The CARICOM Passport

    -The CARICOM passport features the CARICOM logo and the words “Caribbean Community” printed on the cover.

    -The Coat of Arms and the name of the Member State are featured on the cover.

    -It is a national passport for travel within CARICOM and to other countries. It is also one of the measures to promote hassle-free travel for CARICOM nationals.

    -The first CARICOM passport was issued on 7 January 2005. To date, except for The Bahamas, and Montserrat, all other Member States have issued the CARICOM passport

TREATY OF CHAGUARAMAS

The decision to establish the Caribbean Community (CARICOM) was made in November 1972 at the Heads of Government Conference. The Georgetown Accord which sets out the process to establish CARICOM was agreed on in April 1973 The Treaty establishing CARICOM, the Treaty of Chaguaramas, was signed on 4 July 1973. Four Heads of Government (Original Signatories) signed the Treaty: Prime Ministers Errol Barrow for Barbados, Forbes Burnham for Guyana, Michael Manley for Jamaica and Eric Williams for Trinidad and Tobago. What is the CSME?

The CSME is the acronym for the Caribbean Single Market and Economy :

The CSME is a bigger market with better opportunities to produce and sell goods and services :

The CSME provides for :

  • Goods and services to move freely throughout CARICOM
  • The right to establish businesses in Member States without restrictions
  • A common External tariff – a rate of duty applied by all Member States to a product imported from a non-member country
  • Free movement of capital – through measures such as the elimination of foreign exchange controls and the setting up of an integrated capital market, including a Regional stock exchange
  • A common trade policy - internal and international trade agreement and policy
  • Free movement of skills and labour - through measures such as removing all obstacles to intra-regional travel and movement of labour, access to social services, including education and health care, transfer of social security benefits and the setting up of common accreditation and quality standards and measures;
  • Harmonization of laws – for example those pertaining to company and intellectual property law

CARICOM Awards

There are two CARICOM Awards:


The Order of the Caribbean Community (OCC)
the CARICOM Triennial Award for Women.

The OCC is conferred on distinguished CARICOM nationals who have made outstanding contributions to the development of CARICOM.

Conferment of the title The Honourable  and the post-nominals O.C.C. are among several privileges and entitlements given the recipients of the OCC. Other priveleges include:

  •  free movement among Member States of the Community
  • a travel document which is assigned similar status to a diplomatic passport.
  • right to reside in and be gainfully employed in any Member State, as well as the right to acquire and dispose of property, as would citizens of Member States.

There can be no more than 15 awards at any given time and this prestigious honour is not conferred posthumously.
